About


According to European regulations, short interest positions of at least 0.5% (of share capital) have to be published on the websites of the respective authorities. Short positions of at least 0.2% have to be reported to the authorities, but do not have to be published.This means that NOT all short interest notifications are publicly available. The short interest as seen on this website is underestimating the actual short interest and should thus be considered a minimum short interest. A comparison with the average short positions mentioned in a recent ESMA consultation paper (p35-39) suggests that only around 60% of the actual short interest can be derived from the public notifications.

Two rules are implemented to calculate the total short interest by company. As notifications are not (required to be) published when they are below 0.5%, the first rule is that short positions of asset managers in companies are set to 0% whenever the publicly reported position is below 0.5%. The second rule is that short positions older than one year are also set to 0%, to avoid keeping (very) old short positions into the calculation.
